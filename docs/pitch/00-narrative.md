# GOV.UG — pitch narrative

## The story, in one page

Uganda's government speaks with fifty voices online. Every ministry, agency and commission runs its own site, with its own design, its own quirks, and its own risk of being mistaken for a fake. Citizens don't know where to go. Public bodies spend scarce money building and maintaining fragmented WordPress installs. Our international peers — Rwanda, Kenya, South Africa, the UK, Singapore — have long since consolidated onto single, trusted, accessible national platforms.

**GOV.UG is that platform for Uganda.** One unified home for every public body, built on the proven pattern used by gov.uk, canada.ca, gov.sg and digital.govt.nz. One consistent design. One identity. One search. One accessibility floor. One cost base.

We are not starting from scratch. The UK's Government Digital Service has open-sourced ten years of component and pattern work — the GOV.UK Design System, MIT-licensed. Five peer governments already fork and reuse it. Uganda can too.

This document — and the working prototype behind it — shows what GOV.UG looks like, how it would be built, and what the next six months would cost to prove it with two pilot ministries.
