# The ask

We are not asking Cabinet to fund the full programme today. We are asking for three specific things:

## 1. A mandate

A written directive — from either Cabinet, the Office of the Prime Minister, or the Ministry of ICT and National Guidance — that designates a **single accountable owner** (proposed: NITA-U) for the unified national online presence of the Government of Uganda.

Without a clear owner, this initiative will be absorbed into existing siloed procurement. With one, it can move.

## 2. A Phase 0 budget

Approximately **USD 200,000** (≈ UGX 750M at current rates) to fund six months of discovery, team hiring, and pilot design. This is small enough to sit inside the existing NITA-U budget, and corresponds to the cost of maintaining perhaps two ministry WordPress sites for a year.

What this buys:
- The core technical team (7 people) for six months.
- User research across rural and urban cohorts.
- Verification of the MDA register.
- Completion of the technical foundation (this repository is the starting point).
- A pilot specification ready to execute in Phase 1.

## 3. Two pilot partners

Cabinet nomination of **two willing ministries** to serve as Phase 1 pilots. Our recommendation is:

- **Ministry of Health** — high public visibility, active digital team, strong programme-delivery content needs.
- **Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries** — national reach, large rural audience on low-bandwidth connections, ideal for the accessibility and translation tests.

Pilots must be **willing participants** — if the Ministries do not want to do this, the programme will fail. The two above have both expressed interest in consolidated online presence in past NITA-U consultations.

## Decision points we need

1. **Go / no-go on Phase 0 funding** — target decision: within 60 days of pitch.
2. **Go / no-go on pilot partners** — target decision: within 90 days.
3. **Governance charter signed** — target: end of Phase 0.
4. **Phase 1 procurement route agreed** (internal delivery vs. mixed with external partner) — target: end of Phase 0.

## What happens if we do nothing

- MDAs continue building independent sites at higher aggregate cost.
- The accessibility gap widens.
- Uganda falls further behind regional peers (Rwanda's irembo, Kenya's eCitizen, South Africa's gov.za).
- Citizens continue to mistrust ambiguous-looking public websites.
- We remain unable to present a coherent online face of Government to international partners.

None of these are irrecoverable — but each year of inaction compounds them.
