# The precedent — we are not inventing this

## Peer governments have already done it

| Country | Platform | Launched | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| United Kingdom | gov.uk | 2012 | Serves 1 billion+ page views per year. The original and most cited. |
| Canada | canada.ca | 2013 | Bilingual (English / French), fully consolidated. |
| Singapore | gov.sg | 2016 | Integrated with digital identity (Singpass). |
| New Zealand | digital.govt.nz | 2018 | Smaller state, similar pattern. |
| Australia | australia.gov.au | rebuilt 2022 | Latest generation, fork of Australian Government Design System. |
| Rwanda | irembo.gov.rw | 2015 | Closer peer — focused on services more than content. |
| Scotland | mygov.scot | 2014 | Device-first, accessibility-led. |

Every single one of these is publicly documented and most have open-sourced their design system, their content model, or both.

## GOV.UK in particular is a gift

The UK's Government Digital Service has open-sourced nearly everything of value, under MIT / OGL licences that allow commercial and public use:

- `alphagov/govuk-frontend` — ~40 accessible React/Nunjucks components, audited to WCAG 2.2 AA.
- `alphagov/govuk-design-system` — the full pattern library with extensive documentation.
- `alphagov/govuk-prototype-kit` — an Express app for rapid user research prototypes.
- `alphagov/publishing-api` — the content model, including schemas for 80+ document types.

Uganda can **literally fork** these artefacts and rebrand. That is what Canada, Australia and New Zealand all did. It saves 6–12 months of design and development work and, more importantly, delivers a known-good accessibility standard on day one.

## Why this matters for the pitch

The hardest question a Minister can ask is: "will this actually work?" The honest answer is that five peer governments have already proven that it works, and the technology is free and open-sourced for us to use.

We are not asking Cabinet to sponsor an experiment. We are asking them to sponsor an adaptation of a pattern that has been running at national scale, with millions of daily users, in at least seven countries, for a decade.
