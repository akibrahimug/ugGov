# The vision — what "good" looks like

## One home, one voice

GOV.UG is the single online address for the Government of Uganda. Every ministry, agency, commission and programme lives under it with a shared design, a shared search, and a shared publishing system.

From the citizen's perspective:
- A memorable address.
- A consistent header, so they always know they are on a genuine Government site.
- One search box that finds answers across every public body.
- Pages that load fast on a basic Android phone over 3G.
- Content in **English, Luganda and Kiswahili** from day one.
- Step-by-step "how-to-apply" guides — no more hunting across PDFs.

## From the civil servant's perspective

A ministry's communications team logs into **one publishing tool**. They see only the content they own. They write a news article, a guide or an update to their organisation page. A colleague reviews it. They publish it — and it appears on GOV.UG in the shared design, in the shared search, with the correct organisation branding, without a single developer being involved.

The editorial experience is:
- Browser-based, no installation.
- Role-based: you edit your organisation, not others'.
- Two-eyes reviewed: another editor must approve before publishing.
- Versioned: every change is audited and reversible.
- Scheduled: publish at a specific time (useful for embargoed announcements).

## What sits behind it

A simple, modern, boring stack. Three applications, not twelve:

1. A **publishing studio** where editors work.
2. A **frontend site** that citizens read.
3. A **search index** that connects them.

Everything is built on open standards. Nothing ties Uganda to a single vendor. The design system itself is open source and reusable by future projects (local government portals, agency-specific extranets, service-team prototypes).

## The "aha" moment

Open the prototype. Compare it to five current ministry websites on a second screen. The difference is the pitch.
