# The approach — phased, evidence-led delivery

We propose a **phased rollout** that de-risks the programme at every step. No single phase commits Government to a large unrecoverable investment. Each phase delivers something the next phase depends on, and each phase produces something the public can see.

## Phase 0 — Discovery &amp; foundations (months 0–3)

- Governance: formally designate the principal owner (NITA-U / MoICT / OPM) and set up a steering committee.
- User research: structured interviews with citizens across urban and rural cohorts, low-bandwidth device testing.
- Verify the MDA register against the Office of the Prime Minister and Ministry of Public Service.
- Stand up the technical foundation: monorepo, design-system fork, content schemas, Sanity Studio.
- Hire the core team (≈7 people): tech lead, 2 senior fullstack, 1 product designer, 1 content architect, 1 SRE.

**Deliverable:** a running prototype (this repository) plus a governance charter.

## Phase 1 — Pilot with two ministries (months 3–9)

- Onboard **two pilot ministries**: recommended **Ministry of Health** and **Ministry of Agriculture, Animal Industry and Fisheries**. Both are high-visibility, high-traffic, and have credible digital teams to partner with.
- Migrate their existing WordPress content to GOV.UG. Put 301 redirects in place so no inbound links break.
- Add the **Office of the President** landing and **five flagship programmes** (PDM, Emyooga, SAGE, UPE, OWC) with real "how-to-apply" guidance.
- Launch with English + Luganda translations.
- Full editorial training for pilot MDA teams.

**Deliverable:** a publicly visible GOV.UG with ~300–500 real pages, measurable against current ministry sites on speed, accessibility and task completion.

## Phase 2 — Horizontal rollout (months 9–18)

- All **25 cabinet ministries** + top **30 agencies/authorities** + **10 cities**.
- Parliament path: committees, MPs, Hansard, bills tracker.
- Judiciary path: courts, divisions, selected case law.
- Add Kiswahili as a third launch language.

**Deliverable:** ~5,000 pages, matching the "Year 1 MVP" scoping estimate.

## Phase 3 — Local government &amp; depth (months 18–30)

- **146 district** landing pages (autogenerated from structured data, editable override).
- Remaining agencies, commissions, boards.
- Full publications library (Acts, Statutory Instruments, budgets, audits) — ~8,000 items.
- Launch `data.ug.gov` open-data sub-portal.

## Phase 4 — Services &amp; transactions (months 24–36, overlaps Phase 3)

- **100 "how-to-apply" service pages** with structured eligibility and deep links to existing transactional portals (URA, NIRA, NSSF, BoU).
- Introduce decision-tree "smart answer" patterns for common journeys.
- Lightweight transactional features: feedback, subscriptions, access-to-information requests.
- Transactional systems themselves (payments, filings) stay with the existing agency portals — GOV.UG points to them, does not replace them.

## Phase 5 — Sovereignty migration &amp; optimisation (months 36+)

If Phase 1 runs on international cloud for speed of launch, this phase executes the contractually committed migration to fully sovereign hosting (NITA-U National Data Centre or equivalent), with zero service interruption. By this stage the team, the design system, the editorial discipline and the institutional buy-in will all be proven.

## Why this sequencing

- **The pilot de-risks the platform.** Two willing ministries adopting GOV.UG will surface 80% of the real-world issues before we commit to rolling out to 75 more.
- **The migration strategy preserves trust.** 301 redirects and content parity mean citizens don't lose access to anything.
- **The transactional agencies are respected, not subsumed.** URA, NIRA, NSSF, BoU keep their existing investments. We integrate, we don't absorb.
- **Sovereignty is an eventual certainty, not a launch blocker.** Starting on international cloud gets us running in months instead of years, and we migrate inwards once the platform is trusted.
